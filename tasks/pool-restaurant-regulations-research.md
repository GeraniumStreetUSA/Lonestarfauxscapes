## A) TEXAS POOL FENCE/BARRIER CODES

### 1. International Residential Code (IRC) Section R326 - Pool Barriers

**IRC Section R326.4 - Barrier Requirements:**

**Height and Clearance:**
- **Minimum height**: 48 inches (1219 mm) above grade, measured on the side of barrier facing away from pool
- **Maximum bottom clearance**: 2 inches (51 mm) between grade and bottom of barrier
- **Source**: [IRC Section R326 Pool Barrier Specifications](https://www.aahealth.org/sites/default/files/2023-03/Swimming-Pool-Barrier-Requirements.pdf)

**Barrier Composition:**
- Solid barriers without openings cannot have indentations/protrusions (except normal construction tolerances)
- For barriers with horizontal/vertical members where horizontal spacing is less than 45 inches: horizontal members must be on pool side, vertical member spacing maximum 1¾ inches
- For barriers where horizontal spacing is 45 inches or more: vertical member spacing maximum 4 inches
- **Source**: [2015 IRC Pool Requirements](https://www.easthartfordct.gov/sites/g/files/vyhlif9241/f/uploads/2018_section_r326_swimming_pools.pdf)

**Gate Requirements (IRC Section R326.4):**
- Gates must open outward away from pool
- Must be self-closing and self-latching
- Where release mechanism is less than 54 inches from bottom of gate, it must be:
  - Located on pool side of gate
  - 3 inches or more below top of gate
  - Gate/barrier must have no openings greater than ½ inch within 18 inches of release mechanism
- **Source**: [IRC R326.4 Gate Specifications](https://codes.iccsafe.org/s/NYSRC2020P1/part-iii-building-planning-and-construction/NYSRC2020P1-Pt03-Ch03-SecR326.4)

**Additional Requirements:**
- No opening in barrier shall allow passage of 4-inch diameter sphere
- **Source**: [Swimming Pool Barrier Requirements](https://www.aahealth.org/sites/default/files/2023-03/Swimming-Pool-Barrier-Requirements.pdf)

### 2. Texas State Pool Fence Code

**Texas Health and Safety Code Chapter 757 - Pool Yard Enclosures:**

**Applicability:**
- Applies to pools owned, controlled, or maintained by multiunit rental complex owners or property owners associations
- Also applies to doors and windows of rental dwellings opening into pool yards
- **Source**: [Texas Health and Safety Code Chapter 757](https://statutes.capitol.texas.gov/Docs/HS/htm/HS.757.htm)

**Height Requirements:**
- **Minimum height**: 48 inches, measured from ground on side away from pool
- **Source**: [Texas Health and Safety Code Chapter 757](https://statutes.capitol.texas.gov/Docs/HS/htm/HS.757.htm)

**Clearance Requirements:**
- Openings under pool yard enclosure may not allow 4-inch diameter sphere to pass
- **Source**: [Texas Health and Safety Code Chapter 757](https://statutes.capitol.texas.gov/Docs/HS/htm/HS.757.htm)

**Gate Requirements:**
- Must have self-closing and self-latching device
- "Self-closing and self-latching device" defined as: device that causes gate to automatically close without human or electrical power after opening, and automatically latch without human or electrical power when gate closes
- **Source**: [Texas Health and Safety Code Chapter 757](https://statutes.capitol.texas.gov/Docs/HS/htm/HS.757.htm)

**Additional Requirements:**
- Doors and windows opening into pool yard must have alarms
- **Source**: [Texas Pool Fence Laws & Requirements](https://poolguardusa.com/texas-pool-fence-requirements/)

**Penalties:**
- Failure to comply can result in fines ranging from $200 to $1,000 per day
- **Source**: [Texas Pool Fence Laws & Requirements](https://poolguardusa.com/texas-pool-fence-requirements/)

### 4. Texas-Specific Pool Safety Requirements

**General State Requirements (Beyond Chapter 757):**
- All pools must be enclosed by fence at least 48 inches tall
- Fences must be made from non-climbable materials
- **Chain-link fences prohibited** for new pool enclosures constructed after January 1, 1994
- Recommended materials: solid wood, metal, or mesh (not chain-link)
- **Source**: [Texas Pool Fence Laws](https://www.carlsonattorneys.com/texas-pool-fence-laws/)

**Sphere Test:**
- A sphere of 4 inches or more cannot pass under or through pool fence
- Decorative openings must be less than 1¾ inches in all directions
- **Source**: [Texas Pool Fence Laws & Requirements](https://poolguardusa.com/texas-pool-fence-requirements/)

**Gate Requirements:**
- Gates must have self-closing and self-latching functionality
- Must lock by padlock, combination lock, keypad, or key card
- Locks must be out of reach of small children
- **Source**: [Texas Pool Fence Laws & Requirements](https://poolguardusa.com/texas-pool-fence-requirements/)

**Door and Window Requirements:**
- Any door or window leading to pool area must have alarm system
- Alarm must sound when door/window is opened
- **Source**: [Texas Pool Fence Laws & Requirements](https://poolguardusa.com/texas-pool-fence-requirements/)

## B) TEXAS RESTAURANT OUTDOOR PATIO REGULATIONS

### 1. Fire Code Requirements for Restaurant Outdoor Dining Areas

**Assembly Occupancy Classification:**

**Group A-2 Assembly Occupancy:**
- Restaurants with **50 or more people** = "assembly occupancy" (Group A-2)
- Must meet requirements in IBC Chapters 12 (new) and 13 (existing assembly occupancies)
- **Source**: [Restaurant Fire Safety Regulations](https://blog.qrfs.com/144-restaurant-fire-safety-regulations-the-basics/)

**Outdoor Patio Classification:**
- Outdoor areas (pavilions, patios) with occupant load are classified as assembly occupancies even without walls
- Where rooftop contains restaurant with dining seating for 50+ persons, occupied roof = Group A-2 occupancy
- **Source**: [IFC Assembly Occupancy Classification](https://codes.iccsafe.org/s/IBC2018P6/chapter-10-means-of-egress/IBC2018P6-Ch10-Sec1004.7)

---

### 2. International Fire Code - Decorative Materials Requirements

**IFC Chapter 8, Section 807 - Decorative Materials:**

**General Requirements for Assembly Occupancies:**
- Furnishings or decorative materials of explosive or highly flammable character prohibited
- **Source**: [IFC Section 807 Decorative Materials](https://up.codes/s/decorative-materials-and-artificial-decorative-vegetation-in-new-and-existing-bu)

**Flame Resistance Standards (Section 807.5.1):**
- Exposed foam plastic materials used for decorative purposes must have maximum heat release rate of **100 kW** when tested per UL 1975 or NFPA 289
- **Source**: [IFC Chapter 8 Interior Finish](https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IFC2021P1/chapter-8-interior-finish-decorative-materials-and-furnishings)

**Fabric and Textile Decorations (Section 807.4):**
- Curtains, draperies, fabric hangings, and similar combustible decorative materials suspended from walls/ceilings:
  - Must comply with Section 807.4
  - Cannot exceed **10% of specific wall or ceiling area** to which attached (except Group I-3)
- **Source**: [IFC Chapter 8 Interior Finish](https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IFC2021P1/chapter-8-interior-finish-decorative-materials-and-furnishings)

**Flame-Spread Ratings:**
- For walls/ceilings in assembly occupancies: Use materials with **Class A or B flame-spread ratings**
- Recommended: gypsum wallboard, solid wood paneling
- Avoid: carpet, plywood, particle board, plastics, foam plastics (allow fire spread)
- **Source**: [Fire Safety for Assembly Occupancies](https://cms9files.revize.com/marshallmn/_Departments/Public%20Safety/Fire/Fire%20Codes/Assembly-occupancies.pdf)

**Electric Lamps Safety:**
- Standard electric lamps can reach up to **900°F** (sufficient to ignite wood, paper, plastics at ~500°F)
- LED devices recommended as alternative (operate at cooler temperatures)
- **Source**: [Fire Safety for Assembly Occupancies](https://cms9files.revize.com/marshallmn/_Departments/Public%20Safety/Fire/Fire%20Codes/Assembly-occupancies.pdf)

**Motion Picture Screens:**
- Screens in Group A occupancies must meet flame propagation criteria of NFPA 701 Test Method 1 or 2
- **Source**: [IFC Chapter 8 Interior Finish](https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IFC2021P1/chapter-8-interior-finish-decorative-materials-and-furnishings)

---

**IFC Chapter 24/31, Section 2404 - Tents, Canopies, and Membrane Structures:**

**Heating and Cooking Equipment (Section 2404):**
- Heating/AC units and fuel/power sources for cooking must be located at least **10 feet from outside of tents** and membrane structures
- Exception: Solid flammables, butane, or similar devices in spill-proof containers for warming foods/cooking demonstrations (no ignition hazard) are permitted
- **LP-gas containers**: Must be located per Section 2404.16 (generally 10 feet minimum)
- **Source**: [IFC Section 2404 Tents and Canopies](http://www.campusfiresafety.org/Portals/0/Documents/Code%20Corner,%20International%20Code%20Council/Aug11-CodeCorner.pdf)

**Tent Separation:**
- Tents where cooking is performed:
  - Cannot be used for any other purpose
  - Must be separated from other tents/membrane structures by minimum **20 feet**
- **Source**: [IFC Section 2404 Tents and Canopies](http://www.campusfiresafety.org/Portals/0/Documents/Code%20Corner,%20International%20Code%20Council/Aug11-CodeCorner.pdf)

**Flammable Liquids:**
- Flammable liquid-fueled equipment prohibited in tents, canopies, or membrane structures
- Flammable and combustible liquids must be stored outside at least **50 feet** from tents/canopies/membrane structures
- **Source**: [IFC Section 2404 Tents and Canopies](http://www.campusfiresafety.org/Portals/0/Documents/Code%20Corner,%20International%20Code%20Council/Aug11-CodeCorner.pdf)

**LP-Gas Container Requirements:**
- All LP-gas containers must be located outside
- Portable containers ≤500 gallons: **10-foot minimum** separation from structure
- Portable containers >500 gallons: **25-foot minimum** separation from structure
- **Source**: [IFC Section 2404 Tents and Canopies](http://www.campusfiresafty.org/Portals/0/Documents/Code%20Corner,%20International%20Code%20Council/Aug11-CodeCorner.pdf)

---

**Sprinkler Requirements for Assembly Occupancies:**

**Automatic Sprinkler System Required for Group A-2 when:**
1. Fire area exceeds **5,000 square feet**, OR
2. Fire area has occupant load of **100 or more**, OR
3. Fire area is located on floor other than level of exit discharge
- **Source**: [IFC Assembly Occupancy Requirements](https://www.nmcoa.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/12/2018-IBC-and-IFC-Assembly-Spaces-Handout-1.pdf)

---

**Outdoor Cooking on Patios:**

**Charcoal Burners and Open-Flame Cooking:**
- Charcoal burners and open-flame cooking devices prohibited on combustible balconies
- Must maintain **10 feet from combustible construction**
- **Source**: [Travis County Fire Safety Tips](https://www.traviscountytx.gov/fire-marshal/prevention/safety-tips)

**Propane Grills:**
- LP gas burners with LP-gas container water capacity greater than **2.5 pounds**:
  - Prohibited on combustible balconies
  - Must maintain **10 feet from combustible construction**
- **Source**: [Travis County Fire Safety Tips](https://www.traviscountytx.gov/fire-marshal/prevention/safety-tips)

### 4. TABC (Texas Alcoholic Beverage Commission) Requirements

**General TABC Regulatory Framework:**

**Texas Alcoholic Beverage Code:**
- Establishes how alcoholic beverage industry may operate
- TABC adopts Administrative Rules (Title 16, Part 3 of Texas Administrative Code)
- **Source**: [TABC Code and Rules](https://www.tabc.texas.gov/texas-alcohol-laws-regulations/tabc-code-rules/)

**Outdoor Alcohol Service:**
- Bars/restaurants can serve alcohol in outdoor areas
- May need additional permits or approval depending on:
  - Local regulations
  - Specifics of liquor license
- **Source**: [TABC FAQs](https://www.tabc.texas.gov/faqs/)

**Enclosed Patio Requirements (Example - Austin):**
- Beer gardens must be fenced to prevent individuals from passing alcohol from beer garden to areas outside enclosure
- **Source**: [Austin Alcohol Regulations](https://www.austintexas.gov/ace-event-planning-guide/alcohol)

**Important Note:**
- TABC regulations for enclosed outdoor patios vary significantly by local jurisdiction
- State law provides general framework
- Local ordinances provide specific requirements
- **Recommendation**: Contact local TABC office for jurisdiction-specific requirements

**Resources:**
- [TABC Licenses and Permits](https://www.tabc.texas.gov/services/tabc-licenses-permits/)
- [Texas Alcoholic Beverage Code (Full Text)](https://www.tabc.texas.gov/static/sites/default/files/2020-06/mpa-052.pdf)
