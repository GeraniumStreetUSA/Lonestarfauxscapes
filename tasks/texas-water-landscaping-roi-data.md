## 2. TEXAS DROUGHT CONDITIONS AND WATER RESTRICTIONS

### Texas Water Development Board (TWDB) Drought Statistics

**Current Drought Extent (Week of February 10, 2026):**
- 74% of Texas experiencing drought conditions
- Comparison data:
  - Previous week: 69%
  - Three months ago: 54%
  - One year ago: 42%

**Source:** [TWDB Drought Outlook](https://www.twdb.texas.gov/drought/outlook/)

---

**Ongoing Drought Timeline:**
- Current drought began: October 2021
- Peak drought coverage: 97% (July 2022)
- Lowest point: 19% (September 2025)

**Source:** [Texas Water Development Board Drought Program](https://www.twdb.texas.gov/drought/index.asp)

---

**Reservoir Storage (January 2026):**
- Statewide reservoir storage: 73.5% full
- Change from four weeks prior: 74.2% (down 0.7%)
- Approximately 7 percentage points below normal for this time of year

**Source:** [Texas Farm Bureau - Drought Conditions Report](https://texasfarmbureau.org/drought-conditions-persist-across-most-of-texas/)

---

**Future Outlook (Through March 2026):**
- Anticipated drought expansion along Texas coast, West and Central Texas, and Panhandle
- Dry conditions expected to worsen without significant rainfall

**Source:** [TWDB Drought Outlook](https://www.twdb.texas.gov/drought/outlook/index.asp)

---

**Historical Context:**
- At least one serious drought in some part of Texas every decade of the 20th century
- Seven-year drought of record in the 1950s led to formation of TWDB
- 2010-2014 drought: Second worst and second-longest statewide drought

**Source:** [TWDB Drought Comparison Report (1950s vs 2010s)](https://www.twdb.texas.gov/publications/reports/other_reports/doc/Drought-in-Texas-Comparison-1950s-2010s.pdf)

---

### Texas Commission on Environmental Quality (TCEQ) Water Restrictions

**Current Water Restrictions (as of January 2025):**
- Approximately 10% of population served by reporting public water systems are experiencing or threatened by water shortages
- Public water systems self-report water use restrictions since January 1, 2025

**Sources:**
- [TCEQ - List of Texas PWSs Limiting Water Use](https://www.tceq.texas.gov/drinkingwater/trot/droughtw.html)
- [TCEQ - Drought in Texas](https://www.tceq.texas.gov/response/drought)

---

**Drought Response Framework:**
- Under Prior Appropriation Doctrine: "first in time, first in right"
- Senior water rights satisfied before junior water rights
- Junior water rights may be curtailed during shortages
- Temporary water rights first to be curtailed

**Source:** [TCEQ - Water Rights During Drought](https://www.tceq.texas.gov/response/drought/waterrights.html)

## 4. IRRIGATION WATER USAGE FOR LANDSCAPING

### EPA Outdoor Water Use Data

**National Outdoor Water Usage:**
- Landscape irrigation accounts for nearly one-third of all residential water use
- Totals nearly 9 billion gallons per day nationwide
- Residential outdoor water use: Nearly 8 billion gallons per day (mainly landscape irrigation)

**Sources:**
- [EPA WaterSense - US Outdoor Water Use](https://19january2017snapshot.epa.gov/www3/watersense/pubs/outdoor.html)
- [EPA - Outdoors](https://www.epa.gov/watersense/outdoors)

---

**Water Required Per Square Foot:**
- Fundamental conversion: 0.62337 gallons of water covers one square foot with one inch of water
- Annual irrigation goal for efficient use: 12 gallons per square foot of mixed-use landscape per year

**Sources:**
- [University of Florida IFAS - How Much Water Am I Using?](https://edis.ifas.ufl.edu/publication/AE585)
- [DOE/PNNL - Guidelines for Estimating Unmetered Landscaping Water Use](https://www.energy.gov/femp/articles/guidelines-estimating-unmetered-landscaping-water-use)

---

**Annual Irrigation Factor:**
- Represents gallons per square foot required to maintain healthy landscape over 1 year
- Takes into account: growing season, location, plant type, effective precipitation
- Varies significantly by location and climate

**Source:** [EPA - Water Budget Approach](https://www.epa.gov/sites/default/files/2017-01/documents/ws-homes-water-budget-approach.pdf)

---

### USGS Irrigation Water Use Statistics

**Irrigation Scope:**
- USGS estimates include: golf courses, parks, nurseries, turf farms, cemeteries, self-supplied landscape watering

**Source:** [USGS - Irrigation Water Use](https://www.usgs.gov/mission-areas/water-resources/science/irrigation-water-use)

---

**System Efficiency Trends:**
- 10% more irrigated acres using sprinkler systems (2015 vs 2010)
- 19% more irrigated acres using microirrigation systems (2015 vs 2010)
- Both irrigation withdrawals and total irrigated acres increased ~2% between 2010-2015

**Source:** [USGS - Irrigation Water Use](https://www.usgs.gov/mission-areas/water-resources/science/irrigation-water-use)

---

**Data Availability:**
- Water use estimates for 2000-2020 available
- Monthly estimates for irrigation withdrawals and consumptive use
- Data at 12-digit hydrologic unit code level (HUC-12) for conterminous United States

**Source:** [USGS - Water Use in the United States](https://www.usgs.gov/mission-areas/water-resources/science/water-use-united-states)

---

### Texas A&M AgriLife Extension Data

**Texas-Specific Water Usage:**
- In urban areas of Texas, about 25% of water supply used for landscape and garden irrigation

**Source:** [Texas Water Journal - Urban Landscape Water Use in Texas](https://twj-ojs-tdl.tdl.org/twj/article/view/6992)

---

**Available Resources:**
- Landscape Irrigation Efficiency guides
- Residential Irrigation and Water Conservation manuals

- Cycle and Soak irrigation methodology to prevent runoff

**Sources:**

- [Texas A&M AgriLife Extension - Residential Irrigation and Water Conservation](https://agrilifeextension.tamu.edu/asset-external/residential-irrigation-and-water-conservation/)

## 5. PROPERTY VALUE AND TENANT SATISFACTION DATA

### Property Value Impact of Landscaping

**Research-Based Value Increases:**

**Virginia Tech Study:**
- Landscape upgrade from average to excellent: 10-12% increase in home value

**Source:** [Virginia Tech - Effect of Landscape Plants on Perceived Home Value](https://www.pubs.ext.vt.edu/426/426-087/426-087.html)

---

**American Society of Landscape Architects:**
- Landscaping investment increase: 15-20% home value increase
- ROI on landscaping improvements: 100-200% return on investment

**Source:** [Epic Property Management - Landscaping for Multifamily Properties](https://epicpropertymanagement.com/how-landscaping-key-for-multifamily-properties/)

---

**Michigan State University:**
- High-quality landscaping: 5-11% higher sale price vs. average landscaping
- Well-manicured properties: 5.5-12.7% more value than similar homes with minimal landscaping

**Sources:**
- [Zimmerman Mulch - Does Landscaping Increase Home Value?](https://www.zimmermanmulch.com/blog/does-landscaping-increase-home-value/)
- [Natural Green - How Much Does Landscaping Increase Home Value?](https://www.nglawns.com/learn/how-much-does-landscaping-increase-your-home-value/)

---

**Real Estate Agent Perspectives:**
- 97.7% of realtors believe good landscaping improves home value by at least 5%
- 56.3% believe it improves value by 15% or more

**Source:** [Pennsylvania Association of Realtors - Does Landscaping Increase Property Value?](https://www.parealtors.org/blog/does-landscaping-increase-property-value/)

---

**Consensus Range:** Quality landscaping increases property values by 5-20%, with most research clustering around 10-15% for significant improvements.

---

**Negative Impact of Poor Landscaping:**
- Poor landscaping can decrease property values by up to 30%
- Causes negative first impressions and mental devaluation

**Source:** [Coastline Equity - Landscaping and Property Management](https://coastlineequity.net/insights/landscaping-and-property-management-enhancing-property-value-and-tenant-satisfaction-1)

---

### Tenant Satisfaction Related to Landscaping

**First Impressions:**
- Landscaping sets the tone for prospective tenants
- Neatly maintained lawns, vibrant flower beds, and thoughtful walkways convey professional management
- Property appearance directly influences tenant perception of living quality

**Source:** [Coastline Equity - Landscaping and Property Management](https://coastlineequity.net/insights/landscaping-and-property-management-enhancing-property-value-and-tenant-satisfaction-1)

---

**Tenant Retention:**
- Well-designed common areas contribute to tenant satisfaction
- Foster sense of community and offer appealing amenities
- Satisfied tenants more likely to renew leases, minimizing turnover costs

**Source:** [Coastline Equity - Landscaping and Property Management](https://coastlineequity.net/insights/landscaping-and-property-management-enhancing-property-value-and-tenant-satisfaction-1)

---

**Differentiation Factor:**
- Landscaping and outdoor amenities often serve as differentiator between competing properties
- Overgrown lawns and neglected common areas make even well-maintained apartments seem less appealing
- Routine upkeep communicates pride of ownership and care for residents

**Source:** [Epic Property Management - Landscaping for Multifamily Properties](https://epicpropertymanagement.com/how-landscaping-key-for-multifamily-properties/)

---

**Tenant Satisfaction Survey Best Practices:**
- Landscaping and outdoor area upkeep commonly included in satisfaction surveys
- Tenants rate landscaping/outdoor areas on scale of 1-10
- Recommended survey frequency: annual or bi-annual

**Sources:**
- [MCS360 Blog - 7 Ways to Boost Apartment Tenant Satisfaction](https://blog.mcs360.com/7-ways-to-boost-apartment-tenant-satisfaction)
- [Yardi Breeze - 10 Tenant Satisfaction Survey Questions](https://www.yardibreeze.com/blog/2022/12/tenant-satisfaction-survey-questions/)

---
