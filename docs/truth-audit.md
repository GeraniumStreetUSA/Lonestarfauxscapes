# Truth Audit

This document is a decision list, not a deletion plan.

No public site content was removed as part of this audit.

Use this list to decide what stays as-is, what should be rewritten with tighter wording, and what needs owner documentation before it should remain live.

## Audit Method

Claim priority:

1. Repo-backed claims
2. Primary-source external claims
3. Owner-confirmed business facts
4. Unsupported marketing copy

Working rule:

- If a claim is backed by repo content or a primary source, it can stay.
- If a claim is directionally true but too absolute, it should be rewritten.
- If a claim is specific and not backed, it should not stay as written.

## Supported Claims

These have enough backing to keep, though some may still benefit from tighter wording.

### Artificial greenery can be low-maintenance

Status: `Supported`

Reason:

- Repo-wide content consistently describes artificial hedges and living walls as not requiring watering, trimming, or pest treatment.
- Geranium Street product pages also describe the products as low-maintenance and suitable for privacy screens, walls, and decorative use.

Keep logic:

- "No watering"
- "No trimming"
- "No irrigation"
- "Low maintenance" / "minimal maintenance"

### Fire-rated options exist

Status: `Supported`

Reason:

- Geranium Street product pages clearly show fire-rated hedge products and use NFPA 701 Method 2 tested language.
- Repo content already treats fire-rated options as a product path, especially on fire/commercial pages.

Keep logic:

- "Fire-rated options are available"
- "NFPA 701 Method 2 tested foliage is available for projects that require it"
- "Documentation can be requested during quoting or submittals"

Do not overstate:

- Avoid implying every product or every project is automatically fire-rated.

### Decorative-material vs. interior-finish fire-test framing is supported

Status: `Supported`

Reason:

- NFPA 701 is an official flame-propagation standard for textiles and films.
- ASTM E84 is the official surface-burning test method for building materials.
- ASTM's own standard scope says E84 applies to exposed wall and ceiling surfaces.
- ICC chapter references distinguish interior finishes from decorative materials/furnishings.

Keep logic:

- "NFPA 701 and ASTM E84 are different standards"
- "Which standard matters depends on how the product is classified and installed"
- "Decorative-material and interior-finish pathways are not the same question"

### AHJ language is supportable

Status: `Supported`

Reason:

- NFPA's official-definition material defines AHJ as the organization, office, or individual responsible for enforcing a code or approving materials, installations, or procedures.

Keep logic:

- "AHJ" is a valid code-process term
- "requirements can vary based on the enforcing authority"

Do not overstate:

- this supports the use of the term
- it does not prove any specific city approval outcome

Status: `Needs owner confirmation`

Pattern:

- "2-4 days"
- "2-5 days"
- "1-2 weeks"

Problem:

## Unsupported Or Overstated Claims

These should not stay as written unless the owner can produce proof.

### Specific local proof that is not backed by case studies or project records

Status: `Unsupported as written`

Examples:

- `arlington.html`
  - "AT&T Stadium area installations"
  - "We've completed multiple installations near AT&T Stadium, Globe Life Field..."
- `irving.html`
  - "We regularly work with property management companies throughout Las Colinas"
  - "We have experience with multi-story installations in Las Colinas office towers"
- `austin.html`
  - "coordination with Austin fire marshals"

Problem:

- These are direct proof/experience claims.
- Current repo does not contain matching case studies, project logs, project photos with attribution, or owner notes proving them.

Rule:

- Real place names are not proof of completed work there.

### Guarantees

Status: `Unsupported as written`

Examples:

- `frisco.html`
  - "Professional appearance guaranteed"
- `content/blog/2025-12-10-faux-green-walls-vs-real-plants-for-texas-homes.md`
  - "No plant casualties guaranteed"
- older blog/product copy using "guaranteed" lifespan language

Problem:

- Guarantee language creates a promise and raises legal/copy risk.
- No public guarantee policy exists in the repo.

### Exact pricing/cost-comparison math in older blog posts without cited source support

- The post contains detailed plant costs, labor costs, annual water/maintenance costs, total 10-year cost math, and warranty assumptions.

- artificial hedges
- living walls
- low maintenance
- fire-rated options
- UV-rated options
- custom planning
- Texas commercial/residential use cases

The main risk is not the overall service story.

- exact durations
- exact lifespan ranges
- exact warranty terms
- local proof claims without proof

That means this is fixable without gutting the site.
