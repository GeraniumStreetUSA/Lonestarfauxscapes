# Homepage Copy Notes

## Objective

Rewrite the homepage so the first screen explains the offer in plain English:

- Lone Star Faux Scapes installs artificial hedges and living walls across Texas.
- The tone should feel premium and direct, not abstract or ornamental.
- Calls and quote requests should feel lower risk and easier to start.

## Copy Strategy

### 1. Lead with clarity

The hero now states:

- what the company installs
- where it installs
- who it serves

This removes vague headline language and answers the first practical questions quickly.

### 2. Put trust near the first CTA

The trust bar near the hero uses supportable claims already present in the repo/site:

- fire-rated options
- UV-stable materials
- HOA-friendly options
- fast installation
- Texas coverage

Geranium Street USA is positioned as the parent brand and authority signal, not as a separate offer.

### 3. Replace abstract language with job-to-be-done language

The rewrite focuses on the concrete reasons people buy:

- privacy
- low maintenance
- fast installs
- feature walls
- fence extensions
- commercial compliance needs

### 4. Route different buyers clearly

The homepage now gives direct paths for:

- Residential
- Commercial
- Architects / Designers / GCs

This reduces friction for mixed traffic and makes the next click more obvious.

### 5. Keep proof close to decision points

Before users reach the form, the page reinforces:

- custom fabrication
- fire-rated options
- statewide service
- common use cases
- local city coverage

The form intro also tells visitors what to send so the request feels easier to complete.

## Existing Content Reused

The rewrite intentionally reuses strong technical and local themes already in the repo:

- fire-rated options / NFPA 701
- UV-stable materials
- HOA-friendly positioning
- fast install framing
- Texas city coverage
- custom frames and panels
- CAD / shop drawing / spec support language from commercial pages

## Guardrails Kept

The rewrite does **not** add:

- invented reviews
- project counts
- fake urgency
- unsupported guarantees

It also keeps the homepage form IDs and structure intact so the existing contact workflow and related scripts continue to work.

## Expected Outcome

The homepage should now do a better job of:

- reducing uncertainty on the first screen
- improving CTA clarity for phone and form submissions
- showing the business as premium without sounding inflated
- creating cleaner pathways into residential, commercial, and spec-driven pages
